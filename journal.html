Journal

Studio notes, dev writing, and thinking in public.

The journal is the part of the site that turns it from brochure into living studio. The articles are still placeholder-heavy, but that is useful here because long-form filler lets us see how the editorial system behaves before the real cadence starts.

Postmortems Process notes Design essays
Editorial Promise
Useful, not vague

Every entry should reveal something about the work, not just announce that work exists.

Human, not sloppy

The tone should sound like a thoughtful studio notebook, not corporate copywriting.

Connected, not isolated

Articles should feed the case studies, the services, and the credibility of the company itself.

Best article types Postmortems, design notes, release updates, and short reflections from real project work.
Job of the journal Show how the studio thinks, what it learns, and what standards it brings to the work.
Layout test Prove the site can support an editorial rhythm without losing the company feel.
Future use SEO, trust-building, launch support, and a more active studio presence over time.
