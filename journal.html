Journal

Studio notes, dev writing, and thinking in public.

The journal is the part of the site that turns it from brochure into living studio. The articles are still placeholder-heavy, but that helps test an editorial rhythm before the real cadence begins.

Editorial Promise
Useful, not vague

Each entry should reveal something about the work instead of simply announcing that work exists.

Human, not sloppy

The voice should sound like a thoughtful studio notebook rather than generic content marketing.

Connected, not isolated

Articles should support case studies, services, and the credibility of the company itself.

Best formats Essays, postmortems, and field notes

Writing that shows standards, decisions, and lessons rather than polished but empty thought pieces.

Job of the journal Show how the studio thinks

It builds trust, supports SEO, and gives the company a more active and reflective public presence.

Current mode Testing the editorial system

Believable placeholder articles are helping shape the archive before real writing starts arriving.
