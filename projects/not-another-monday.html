Product Snapshot

Built for Chooky Chasing Life with a soft, premium, nature-inspired direction that feels supportive rather than clinical.

The brief

Not Another Monday was built for Chooky Chasing Life as a habit, health, and self-progress app that helps people stay consistent with the basics that improve their life. The product needed to bring structure and encouragement together in one place without feeling like a spreadsheet, a medical dashboard, or an overbuilt fitness app.

At the center of the experience is one simple question: "Did you move your life forward today?" From there, the app turns daily action into something visible, supportive, and worth coming back to.

What the app does

The app gives users a clear daily view of what matters most, then helps them track those actions with minimal taps, clean progress, and a grounded visual style.

  • Daily habit tracking, streak-building, and simple progress check-ins.
  • Weight, body progress, hydration, movement, fasting, and stretching support.
  • One-line journaling, daily reflection, and motivational summaries.
  • Progress photos, measurements, dashboards, and shareable progress moments.
  • Onboarding, profile setup, personalized goals, unit preferences, and account syncing.

The product direction

A big part of the work was making sure the product felt emotionally right. People are not just logging numbers in an app like this. They are trying to build trust with themselves, hold momentum, and stay steady on ordinary days. The design had to support that feeling.

Core design principles

  • Make consistency feel calm, achievable, and rewarding.
  • Reduce friction with minimal taps, clear hierarchy, and generous spacing.
  • Celebrate momentum instead of pushing perfection.

The build challenge

One of the hardest parts of the project was making a feature-rich app feel simple on the surface. Habits, weight, water, journaling, streaks, onboarding, authentication, syncing, and cross-platform behavior all had to coexist inside a cleaner, calmer experience.

The build also needed to hold up across iOS and Android. That meant working through layout issues on smaller phones, account and sync behavior, platform-specific release details, and the challenge of making progress feel motivating rather than mechanical.

Why this project mattered

Not Another Monday is worth highlighting not only because of the product itself, but because it was our first paid commissioned build. It was an important step in moving from internal ideas into client work, learning how to shape another entity's vision into a real product while keeping the build thoughtful, usable, and aligned.

That kind of collaboration changes the job in a good way. It sharpens communication, priorities, and accountability. It also teaches you how much trust matters when you are building something that represents more than your own brand.
